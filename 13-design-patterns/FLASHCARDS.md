# Design Patterns Flashcards

## Q1: What is a design pattern?
**A**: A proven solution to a recurring software design problem. A template for solving common problems.

---

## Q2: What are the three categories of GoF patterns?
**A**: Creational (object creation), Structural (object composition), Behavioral (object interaction)

---

## Q3: When should you use Singleton?
**A**: When exactly one instance is needed and global access is required.

---

## Q4: What does Factory Method pattern do?
**A**: Defines interface for creating objects, lets subclasses decide which class to instantiate.

---

## Q5: What is the difference between Factory and Abstract Factory?
**A**: Factory creates one type of product. Abstract Factory creates families of related products.

---

## Q6: When use Adapter pattern?
**A**: When you need to make incompatible interfaces work together.

---

## Q7: What is Decorator pattern used for?
**A**: Adding responsibilities to objects dynamically. Alternatives to subclassing.

---

## Q8: When use Observer pattern?
**A**: When changes in one object need to be communicated to other objects (pub/sub).

---

## Q9: When use Strategy pattern?
**A**: When you need to swap algorithms or behaviors at runtime.

---

## Q10: What is Builder pattern good for?
**A**: Creating complex objects step by step, especially with many optional parameters.

---

## Q11: What does Facade pattern provide?
**A**: Simplified interface to complex subsystem. Hides complexity.

---

## Q12: What is the difference between Composition and Aggregation?
**A**: Composition: child cannot exist without parent. Aggregation: child can exist independently.

---

## Q13: When use Proxy pattern?
**A**: When you need to control access to an object (protection, lazy loading, remote).

---

## Q14: What is Command pattern?
**A**: Encapsulates request as an object, allowing parameterization and queuing.

---

## Q15: Why avoid overusing Singleton?
**A**: Creates global state, hard to test, hidden dependencies, tight coupling.
