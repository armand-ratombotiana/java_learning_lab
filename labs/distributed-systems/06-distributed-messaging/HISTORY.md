# History of Distributed Messaging

## Timeline
- **2004**: AMQP specification work begins
- **2007**: RabbitMQ released
- **2011**: Apache Kafka created at LinkedIn by Jay Kreps
- **2012**: Kafka open-sourced
- **2016**: Apache Pulsar open-sourced by Yahoo
- **2018**: Kafka Streams GA

## Key Figures
- **Jay Kreps**: Kafka creator (LinkedIn/Confluent)
- **Neha Narkhede**: Kafka co-creator, Confluent co-founder
- **Matthias Radestock**: RabbitMQ lead developer
- **Joe Francis**: Pulsar creator (Yahoo)

## Evolution
From message queues (RabbitMQ) to distributed log (Kafka) to cloud-native messaging (Pulsar), the space evolves toward higher scale, lower latency, and better operational characteristics.
