# History of Time and Ordering

## Timeline
- **1978**: Lamport publishes "Time, Clocks, and the Ordering of Events in a Distributed System"
- **1988**: Vector clocks proposed by Fidge and Mattern
- **1991**: Interval Tree Clocks (efficient vector clocks)
- **2008**: TrueTime used in Google Spanner
- **2014**: Hybrid Logical Clocks (Kulkarni et al.)
- **2017**: Amazon Time Sync Service (precision clock)

## Key Figures
- **Leslie Lamport**: Lamport clocks, logical time pioneer
- **Colin Fidge**: Vector clocks
- **Friedemann Mattern**: Vector clocks
- **Kulkarni et al.**: Hybrid Logical Clocks

## Evolution
From pure logical clocks (Lamport) to hybrid approaches (HLC) that combine logical and physical time for the best of both worlds.
