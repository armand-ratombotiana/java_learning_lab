# History of Consistency Models

## Timeline
- **1979**: Lamport defines sequential consistency
- **1986**: Dubois, Scheurich, Briggs formalize memory consistency
- **1990**: Herlihy and Wing define linearizability
- **2004**: Amazon Dynamo popularizes eventual consistency
- **2007**: GOOG Spanner achieves external consistency
- **2012**: Lloyd et al. propose causal consistency in COPS

## Key Figures
- **Leslie Lamport** - Sequential consistency, distributed systems pioneer
- **Maurice Herlihy** - Linearizability, concurrent algorithms
- **Jeannette Wing** - Linearizability formalization
- **Prince Mahajan** - Consistency model classification

## Evolution
From strict formal models to practical implementations, consistency models now guide billions of dollars in database and storage system architecture.
