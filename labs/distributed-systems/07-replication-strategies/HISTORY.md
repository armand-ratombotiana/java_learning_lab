# History of Replication

## Timeline
- **1970s**: Database replication in IBM IMS
- **1990s**: Tandem NonStop SQL (multi-leader)
- **2000s**: MySQL binlog replication
- **2004**: Amazon Dynamo (leaderless, gossip)
- **2006**: Google Bigtable (single-leader, GFS)
- **2012**: Google Spanner (global, TrueTime)

## Key Figures
- **Werner Vogels**: Dynamo, eventually consistent replication
- **Giuseppe DeCandia**: Dynamo paper lead author
- **Mike Burrows**: Chubby/GFS replication

## Evolution
From simple primary-backup to complex multi-leader and leaderless protocols, replication strategies have evolved to meet scale and consistency demands.
