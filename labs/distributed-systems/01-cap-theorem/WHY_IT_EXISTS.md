# Why CAP Theorem Exists

## Historical Context
Eric Brewer presented the CAP theorem as a conjecture in 2000 at the ACM Symposium on Principles of Distributed Computing. It was formally proven by Seth Gilbert and Nancy Lynch in 2002.

## Purpose
The CAP theorem exists to:
1. Provide a framework for reasoning about tradeoffs in distributed systems
2. Help architects make informed decisions about system design
3. Explain why distributed databases behave differently from single-node databases
4. Guide technology selection based on application requirements

## Real-World Motivation
As web-scale applications grew (Google, Amazon, eBay), single-node databases became bottlenecks. Engineers needed theoretical guidance for building systems that span multiple data centers.
