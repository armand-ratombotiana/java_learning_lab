# History of the CAP Theorem

## Timeline
- **2000**: Eric Brewer presents CAP as a conjecture at PODC
- **2002**: Gilbert and Lynch publish formal proof
- **2004**: Amazon Dynamo paper (AP system) cites CAP
- **2006**: Google Bigtable paper (CP system) references CAP
- **2012**: Brewer clarifies CAP misconceptions in follow-up paper
- **2017**: PACELC extension proposed by Abadi

## Key Figures
- **Eric Brewer** - UC Berkeley professor, Google VP Infrastructure
- **Nancy Lynch** - MIT professor, distributed computing theorist
- **Seth Gilbert** - National University of Singapore, formalized proof
- **Daniel Abadi** - Extended CAP with PACELC tradeoff

## Evolution
The CAP theorem evolved from informal observation to formal theorem to practical design guideline. Modern interpretations emphasize that CAP tradeoffs exist on a continuum rather than binary choices.
