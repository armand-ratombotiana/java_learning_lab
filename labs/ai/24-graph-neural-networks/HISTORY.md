# History of Graph Neural Networks

## 1940s-1960s: Foundations
- McCulloch-Pitts neuron model (1943)
- Hebbian learning rule (1949)
- Perceptron algorithm (Rosenblatt, 1957)
- ADALINE (Widrow-Hoff, 1960)

## 1969-1979: First AI Winter
- Perceptrons book (Minsky-Papert) showed limitations
- Reduced funding and interest

## 1980s: Renaissance
- Backpropagation popularized (Rumelhart, Hinton, Williams, 1986)
- Connectionism and parallel distributed processing
- Neural networks for practical applications

## 1990s: Statistical Learning
- Support Vector Machines (Vapnik)
- Ensemble methods (Random Forests, Boosting)
- Bayesian networks and graphical models

## 2000s: Deep Learning Precursors
- Convolutional Neural Networks (LeCun, 1998)
- Long Short-Term Memory (Hochreiter, 1997)
- Restricted Boltzmann Machines (Hinton, 2002)

## 2010s: Deep Learning Revolution
- AlexNet wins ImageNet (2012) - deep CNNs
- DQN plays Atari (DeepMind, 2013)
- Generative Adversarial Networks (Goodfellow, 2014)
- Transformer architecture (Vaswani et al., 2017)
- BERT and GPT (2018) - pre-trained language models

## 2020s: Foundation Models
- GPT-3, GPT-4, Claude, Gemini - large language models
- DALL-E, Stable Diffusion - text-to-image generation
- Scaling laws: performance improves with compute, data, model size
- Focus on alignment, safety, and responsible AI
