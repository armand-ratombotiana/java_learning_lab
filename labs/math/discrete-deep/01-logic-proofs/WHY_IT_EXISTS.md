# Why It Exists: Logic and Proofs

## The Problem It Solves
Before the formal development of Logic and Proofs, practitioners relied on approximate methods with unknown error bounds, intuitive reasoning without rigor, and ad-hoc techniques that did not generalize across problems.

### The Solution
Logic and Proofs provides precise mathematical definitions, provably correct methods, error bounds and convergence guarantees, and a unified framework for diverse problems.

## Design Rationale
The formulation minimizes assumptions while maximizing applicability, enables rigorous analysis, supports efficient implementation, and provides clear error bounds.

## Alternative Approaches
1. **Empirical methods**: Less rigorous, harder to validate
2. **Symbolic computation**: More precise but slower
3. **Analog computation**: Less flexible, harder to scale

The winning approach offers the best balance of rigor, efficiency, and flexibility.
