# Mental Models: Number Theory

## Conceptual Frameworks

### 1. The Geometric View
number theory often has geometric interpretations. Visualizing vectors, surfaces, and transformations builds intuition for abstract concepts.

### 2. The Information View
Information and optimization problems can be understood through the lens of uncertainty reduction and efficient encoding.

### 3. The Computational View
Every mathematical operation has a computational cost. Understanding complexity trade-offs guides algorithm selection.

## Analogies

### The Landscape Metaphor
Optimization is like hiking in a mountain range: gradient descent is following the steepest downhill path; Newton's method is using a telescope to see the curvature.

### The Communication Metaphor
Information theory quantifies how much you can learn from a message. Channel capacity is the maximum throughput of a communication pipe.

### The Precision Toolbox
Numerical methods are like measuring tools: each has different precision, range, and cost. Choose the right tool for the job.

## Thinking Tools

1. **Start simple**: Test with toy problems before real data
2. **Visualize**: Plot functions, distributions, and convergence
3. **Check sanity**: Does the output make sense?
4. **Compare methods**: Different algorithms for the same problem
5. **Profile**: Where does the time go?
6. **Test edge cases**: Boundaries reveal weaknesses
