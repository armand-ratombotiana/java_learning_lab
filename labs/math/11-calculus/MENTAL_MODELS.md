# Mental Models for Calculus

## The Tangent Line (Derivative)

The derivative $f'(x)$ is the slope of the line that just touches the curve at $x$.

The slope of the curve at point = slope of tangent line = instantaneous rate of change.

## The Area Under the Curve (Integral)

The integral $\int_a^b f(x)\,dx$ is the total area between $f(x)$ and the x-axis from $x=a$ to $x=b$.

## The Limit

A limit describes what happens as we get arbitrarily close to a point. The derivative is the limit of slopes of secant lines as the two points converge.

## The Gradient (Multivariable)

The gradient $\nabla f$ points in the direction of steepest ascent. Following the negative gradient is how machine learning optimizes.

## The Fundamental Theorem of Calculus

Differentiation and integration are inverse operations. This connects rates of change to total accumulation.
