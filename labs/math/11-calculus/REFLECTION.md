# Reflection on Calculus

## Self-Assessment

- Can I compute derivatives and integrals analytically for basic functions?
- Do I understand the relationship between derivatives and rates of change?
- Can I implement numerical differentiation and integration?
- Do I understand gradient descent and its role in ML?
- Can I apply the chain rule to composite functions?

## Growth Areas

- **Multivariable calculus**: partial derivatives, gradients, Jacobians
- **Vector calculus**: divergence, curl, gradient theorem
- **Differential equations**: ODEs, PDEs, numerical solvers
- **Calculus of variations**: function optimization, Euler-Lagrange
- **Complex analysis**: analytic functions, contour integration

## Key Insight

Calculus provides the mathematical framework for understanding change. From physics to machine learning, from economics to biology, wherever there is change there is calculus. The Fundamental Theorem of Calculus — connecting differentiation and integration — is one of the most profound insights in mathematics.
