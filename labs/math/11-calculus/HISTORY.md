# History of Calculus

## Timeline

| Year | Development |
|------|------------|
| ~250 BCE | Archimedes: method of exhaustion (precursor to integration) |
| 1630s | Fermat: tangent lines, maxima/minima |
| 1660s | Newton: fluxions (derivatives) and fluent (integrals) |
| 1684 | Leibniz: publishes differential calculus notation ($dy/dx$, $\int$) |
| 1696 | L'Hôpital: textbook on calculus |
| 1730s | Euler: formalizes functions, series, calculus of variations |
| 1820s | Cauchy: rigorous limits, continuity, convergence |
| 1850s | Riemann: formal definition of the integral |
| 1900s | Lebesgue: measure theory, Lebesgue integral |

## Key Figures

- **Isaac Newton** (1643–1727) — calculus, laws of motion
- **Gottfried Wilhelm Leibniz** (1646–1716) — notation, independent invention
- **Augustin-Louis Cauchy** (1789–1857) — rigorous analysis
- **Bernhard Riemann** (1826–1866) — Riemann integral
- **Henri Lebesgue** (1875–1941) — measure theory
