# Reflection on Probability

## Self-Assessment

- Can I compute conditional probabilities using Bayes' theorem?
- Do I understand expectation, variance, and covariance?
- Can I distinguish between discrete and continuous distributions?
- Do I know when to apply the Law of Large Numbers and CLT?
- Can I implement Monte Carlo simulations correctly?

## Growth Areas

- **Stochastic processes**: Markov chains, random walks, Brownian motion
- **Bayesian inference**: MCMC, variational inference
- **Information theory**: entropy, KL divergence, mutual information
- **Probabilistic programming**: Stan, PyMC, Figaro

## Key Insight

Probability is not just about gambling — it's the mathematical foundation for learning from data, making predictions, and reasoning about uncertainty. In a world of incomplete information, probability is our most powerful tool.
