# Reflection on Arithmetic

## Self-Assessment Questions

- Can I add, subtract, multiply, and divide in multiple number systems (binary, hex)?
- Do I understand when integer overflow occurs and how to prevent it?
- Can I choose the right numeric type (`int` vs `long` vs `BigDecimal`)?
- Do I know how floating-point rounding works and when to use `BigDecimal`?
- Can I implement arithmetic algorithms (long addition, GCD, prime factorization) from scratch?

## Growth Areas

- **Fixed-point arithmetic**: used in financial systems
- **Arbitrary precision**: BigInteger/BigDecimal internals (Karatsuba, Toom-Cook, FFT multiplication)
- **Hardware awareness**: understand instruction-level parallelism, FMA, SIMD

## Key Insight

Arithmetic is not just computation — it's the bridge between abstract mathematics and physical reality. Every digital circuit, every algorithm, every program ultimately reduces to arithmetic operations on bits.
