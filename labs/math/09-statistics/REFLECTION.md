# Reflection on Statistics

## Self-Assessment

- Can I compute descriptive statistics and interpret them?
- Do I understand the difference between descriptive and inferential statistics?
- Can I perform hypothesis tests (t-test, chi-squared)?
- Do I know when to use linear regression and how to evaluate it?
- Can I identify common statistical fallacies (correlation ≠ causation, Simpson's paradox)?

## Growth Areas

- **Bayesian statistics**: prior, likelihood, posterior, MCMC
- **Time series**: ARIMA, seasonality, forecasting
- **Experimental design**: power analysis, randomization, blocking
- **Causal inference**: do-calculus, instrumental variables
- **Multivariate statistics**: MANOVA, factor analysis

## Key Insight

Statistics is the bridge between data and knowledge. It provides the tools to quantify uncertainty, test hypotheses, and make predictions. In the age of machine learning, statistical thinking is more important than ever — every ML model is ultimately a statistical model.
