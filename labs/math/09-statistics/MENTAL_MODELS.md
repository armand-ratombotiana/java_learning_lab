# Mental Models for Statistics

## The Distribution Shape

Visualize data as a histogram. Shape tells you about central tendency, spread, skewness, and outliers.

```
Normal:    ╱╲
Skewed:  ╱╱╲
Uniform: ────
```

## The Mean as Balance Point

The mean is the point where the histogram would balance. The median is the midpoint where half the data is on each side.

## The Standard Deviation Ruler

In a normal distribution:
- 68% of data within $\pm 1\sigma$
- 95% within $\pm 2\sigma$
- 99.7% within $\pm 3\sigma$

## The Correlation Lens

Correlation measures linear relationship from -1 (perfect inverse) to +1 (perfect direct). $\rho = 0$ means no linear relationship.

## The Confidence Interval

A 95% confidence interval means: if we repeated the experiment many times, 95% of intervals would contain the true parameter.

## The Null Hypothesis

The default assumption (usually "no effect"). $p$-value = probability of observing data as extreme or more, assuming null is true.
