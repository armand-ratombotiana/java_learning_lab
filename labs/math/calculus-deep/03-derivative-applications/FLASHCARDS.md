# Flashcards: Derivative Applications

## Front-Back Cards

### Card 1
**Front**: What is the fundamental definition of Derivative Applications?
**Back**: The formal definition involves specific mathematical operations that transform inputs according to established rules, providing a rigorous framework for analysis.

### Card 2
**Front**: What are the key properties of Derivative Applications?
**Back**: The core properties include continuity, differentiability, convergence guarantees, stability characteristics, and error bounds.

### Card 3
**Front**: What is the time complexity of the standard algorithm?
**Back**: O(n) for the standard implementation, with O(1) additional space for iterative versions.

### Card 4
**Front**: What numerical issues commonly arise?
**Back**: Floating-point rounding errors, catastrophic cancellation from subtracting nearly equal numbers, and ill-conditioned inputs.

### Card 5
**Front**: Name three real-world applications.
**Back**: Scientific computing, engineering analysis, and machine learning algorithms.

### Card 6
**Front**: How do you verify implementation correctness?
**Back**: Unit tests, property-based testing, comparison with known analytical results, and convergence analysis.

### Card 7
**Front**: What is the fundamental trade-off in algorithm design?
**Back**: Speed vs. accuracy vs. numerical stability -- no algorithm excels at all three.

### Card 8
**Front**: Which design patterns are commonly used in math implementations?
**Back**: Strategy pattern for algorithm selection, Template Method for common skeleton, Factory for instance creation.

### Card 11
Higher precision arithmetic always produces more accurate results. **Answer**: False -- higher precision can mask but does not eliminate fundamental numerical issues.

## Key Terms
- **Core concept**: The primary mathematical object and its definition
- **Algorithm**: Step-by-step computational procedure
- **Convergence**: Approaching a limit as iterations increase
- **Stability**: Insensitivity to small perturbations in input
- **Conditioning**: Sensitivity of output to changes in input
