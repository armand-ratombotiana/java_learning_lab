# Geometry: Shapes, Areas, Volumes, and Transformations

## 1. Euclidean Geometry Fundamentals

### 1.1 Basic Definitions
- **Point**: Zero-dimensional location in space
- **Line**: Infinite set of points extending in both directions
- **Plane**: Flat 2D surface extending infinitely
- **Angle**: Two rays with common endpoint

### 1.2 Angle Types
- Acute: 0° < θ < 90°
- Right: θ = 90°
- Obtuse: 90° < θ < 180°
- Straight: θ = 180°
- Reflex: 180° < θ < 360°

### 1.3 Parallel Lines
- Corresponding angles equal
- Alternate interior angles equal
- Same-side interior angles supplementary

## 2. Triangle Geometry

### 2.1 Triangle Types
- Equilateral: All sides equal, all angles 60°
- Isosceles: Two sides equal, base angles equal
- Scalene: All sides different
- Right: One angle 90°
- Acute: All angles < 90°
- Obtuse: One angle > 90°

### 2.2 Triangle Properties
- Sum of angles: α + β + γ = 180°
- Pythagorean theorem: a² + b² = c² (right triangles)
- Area = (1/2)bh where b = base, h = height

### 2.3 Similar Triangles
- Corresponding angles equal
- Sides in proportion
- Area ratio = (scale factor)²

## 3. Circle Geometry

### 3.1 Circle Definitions
- **Radius**: Distance from center to any point on circle
- **Diameter**: 2 × radius
- **Chord**: Line connecting two points on circle
- **Tangent**: Line touching circle at one point

### 3.2 Circle Formulas
- Circumference: C = 2πr
- Area: A = πr²
- Arc length: L = rθ (θ in radians)
- Sector area: A = (1/2)r²θ

### 3.3 Circle Theorems
- Angle at center = 2 × angle at circumference
- Angles in same segment are equal
- Opposite angles in cyclic quadrilateral sum to 180°

## 4. Quadrilaterals

### 4.1 Types
- **Square**: All sides equal, all angles 90°
- **Rectangle**: Opposite sides equal, all angles 90°
- **Parallelogram**: Opposite sides parallel
- **Rhombus**: All sides equal, opposite angles equal
- **Trapezoid**: One pair parallel sides
- **Kite**: Two pairs adjacent equal sides

### 4.2 Area Formulas
- Square: A = s²
- Rectangle: A = l × w
- Parallelogram: A = b × h
- Trapezoid: A = (1/2)(a + b)h
- Rhombus: A = (d₁ × d₂)/2

## 5. Polygons

### 5.1 Regular Polygons
- Interior angle = (n-2) × 180°/n
- Exterior angle = 360°/n
- Area = (1/4)n·s²·cot(π/n)

### 5.2 Common Polygons
- Triangle: 3 sides
- Quadrilateral: 4 sides
- Pentagon: 5 sides
- Hexagon: 6 sides
- Octagon: 8 sides
- Decagon: 10 sides

## 6. 3D Geometry

### 6.1 Prisms
- **Rectangular Prism**: V = l × w × h, SA = 2(lw + lh + wh)
- **Cube**: V = s³, SA = 6s²
- **Triangular Prism**: V = (1/2)bh × l

### 6.2 Pyramids
- **Square Pyramid**: V = (1/3)b²h
- **Triangular Pyramid (Tetrahedron)**: V = (1/3) × base area × h

### 6.3 Curved Solids
- **Cylinder**: V = πr²h, SA = 2πr(r + h)
- **Cone**: V = (1/3)πr²h, SA = πr(r + √(r² + h²))
- **Sphere**: V = (4/3)πr³, SA = 4πr²
- **Hemisphere**: V = (2/3)πr³, SA = 3πr²

## 7. Coordinate Geometry

### 7.1 Distance Formula
d = √[(x₂-x₁)² + (y₂-y₁)²]

### 7.2 Midpoint Formula
M = ((x₁+x₂)/2, (y₁+y₂)/2)

### 7.3 Slope
m = (y₂-y₁)/(x₂-x₁)

### 7.4 Line Equations
- Point-slope: y - y₁ = m(x - x₁)
- Slope-intercept: y = mx + b
- Two-point: (y - y₁)/(x - x₁) = (y₂-y₁)/(x₂-x₁)

## 8. Conic Sections

### 8.1 Circle
(x - h)² + (y - k)² = r²

### 8.2 Ellipse
(x - h)²/a² + (y - k)²/b² = 1

### 8.3 Parabola
y = ax² + bx + c or x = ay² + by + c

### 8.4 Hyperbola
(x - h)²/a² - (y - k)²/b² = 1

## 9. Transformations

### 9.1 Rigid Transformations
- **Translation**: (x, y) → (x + a, y + b)
- **Rotation**: About origin by angle θ
- **Reflection**: Across line or point

### 9.2 Non-rigid Transformations
- **Dilation**: (x, y) → (kx, ky) where k is scale factor
- **Stretch**: In one direction
- **Shear**: Parallel shift proportional to distance

### 9.3 Transformation Matrices
- Identity: [[1,0],[0,1]]
- Scale: [[s,0],[0,s]]
- Rotation: [[cos θ, -sin θ],[sin θ, cos θ]]
- Reflection (x-axis): [[1,0],[0,-1]]

## 10. Trigonometric Applications in Geometry

### 10.1 Law of Sines
sin(A)/a = sin(B)/b = sin(C)/c

### 10.2 Law of Cosines
c² = a² + b² - 2ab·cos(C)

### 10.3 Heron's Formula
A = √[s(s-a)(s-b)(s-c)] where s = (a+b+c)/2

## 11. Vector Geometry

### 11.1 Vector Operations
- Addition: v + w = (v₁+w₁, v₂+w₂)
- Scalar multiplication: kv = (kv₁, kv₂)
- Dot product: v·w = v₁w₁ + v₂w₂ = |v||w|cos θ
- Cross product (3D): v × w = perpendicular vector

### 11.2 Applications
- Projection of one vector onto another
- Finding angles between lines/planes
- Area calculations using cross product
