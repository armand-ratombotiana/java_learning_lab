# History of Geometry

## Timeline

| Period | Development |
|--------|------------|
| ~3000 BCE | Egyptians use geometry for pyramid construction |
| ~600 BCE | Thales introduces deductive geometry |
| ~300 BCE | Euclid's *Elements* — axiomatic geometry |
| ~250 BCE | Archimedes: area of circle, volume of sphere |
| 1637 CE | Descartes: analytic geometry (coordinates) |
| 1829 CE | Lobachevsky: hyperbolic (non-Euclidean) geometry |
| 1854 CE | Riemann: elliptic geometry, curved spaces |
| 1915 CE | Einstein: general relativity — spacetime curvature |
| 1970s | Computational geometry becomes a field |

## Key Figures

- **Euclid** (c. 300 BCE) — axiomatic geometry for 2000+ years
- **René Descartes** (1596–1650) — coordinates unite algebra and geometry
- **Carl Friedrich Gauss** (1777–1855) — differential geometry, curved surfaces
- **Bernhard Riemann** (1826–1866) — Riemannian geometry (foundation of GR)
