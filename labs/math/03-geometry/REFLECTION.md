# Reflection on Geometry

## Self-Assessment

- Can I compute distances, areas, intersections analytically?
- Do I understand coordinate systems and transformations?
- Can I implement point-in-polygon, convex hull, closest pair?
- Do I understand the relationship between geometry and linear algebra?
- Can I reason about floating-point precision in geometric algorithms?

## Growth Areas

- **Differential geometry**: curvature, manifolds
- **Topology**: properties preserved under continuous deformation
- **Computational geometry**: robust algorithms for geometric problems
- **3D geometry**: quaternions, meshes, signed distance fields

## Key Insight

Geometry bridges the abstract (axioms, proofs) and the tangible (measurement, construction). In computing, geometry is the language of spatial reasoning — from game physics to robotics to scientific simulation.
