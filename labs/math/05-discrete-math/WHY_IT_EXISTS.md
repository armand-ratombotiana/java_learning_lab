# Why Discrete Mathematics Exists

Discrete math exists because many real-world phenomena are inherently countable and non-continuous.

## Core Motivations

- **Computer science**: computers are finite-state machines operating on discrete data (bits)
- **Logic**: reasoning about truth, validity, and proof
- **Counting**: arrangements, selections, and probability
- **Number theory**: cryptography, hashing, random number generation
- **Graph theory**: networks, relationships, optimization

Discrete math provides the theoretical foundation for all of computer science — algorithms, data structures, computability, and complexity.
