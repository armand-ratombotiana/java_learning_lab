# Reflection on Discrete Mathematics

## Self-Assessment

- Can I prove a simple statement by induction?
- Do I understand De Morgan's laws and can I apply them?
- Can I compute GCD, LCM, and modular inverse?
- Do I understand the relationship between sets and boolean algebra?
- Can I distinguish necessary vs sufficient conditions?

## Growth Areas

- **Computability**: Turing machines, undecidability
- **Complexity theory**: P vs NP, reductions
- **Model checking**: verifying system properties with temporal logic
- **Type theory**: the Curry-Howard isomorphism (proofs as programs)

## Key Insight

Discrete math is the mathematics of computation. Every program is a proof, every data structure is a discrete object, every algorithm is a constructive proof of a theorem. Understanding discrete math is understanding computer science itself.
