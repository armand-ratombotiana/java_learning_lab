# History of Algebra

## Timeline

| Period | Development |
|--------|------------|
| ~1900 BCE | Babylonian clay tablets solve quadratic equations |
| ~300 CE | Diophantus' *Arithmetica* — early symbolic algebra |
| 820 CE | Al-Khwarizmi's *Al-Jabr* — systematic solution of linear & quadratic equations |
| 1545 CE | Cardano publishes solution to cubic equations |
| 1637 CE | Descartes connects algebra to geometry (Cartesian coordinates) |
| 1799 CE | Gauss proves Fundamental Theorem of Algebra |
| 1843 CE | Hamilton invents quaternions |
| 1854 CE | Boole's algebra of logic — foundation of computing |
| 1930s | Abstract algebra: groups, rings, fields formalized |

## Key Figures

- **Al-Khwarizmi** (780–850) — father of algebra, algorithm namesake
- **René Descartes** (1596–1650) — analytic geometry, notation
- **Évariste Galois** (1811–1832) — group theory, solvability of equations
