# Reflection on Algebra

## Self-Assessment

- Can I solve linear and quadratic equations analytically?
- Do I understand matrix multiplication and its geometric meaning (composition of transformations)?
- Can I implement Gaussian elimination and understand pivoting?
- Do I recognize algebraic structures (groups, rings, fields) in code (generics, interfaces)?
- Can I analyze algorithm complexity using algebraic growth rates?

## Growth Areas

- **Abstract algebra**: group theory, Galois theory
- **Computational linear algebra**: SVD, QR decomposition, eigenvalues
- **Algebraic geometry**: solving systems of polynomial equations
- **Category theory**: the most abstract algebra of all

## Key Insight

Algebra is not just solving for $x$ — it's the study of structure. Every time you define an interface with operations and laws, you're doing algebra.
