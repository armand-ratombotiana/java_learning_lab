# Why Algebra Exists

Algebra was invented to generalize arithmetic — to solve problems where quantities are unknown. The word comes from Arabic *al-jabr* ("reunion of broken parts") from Al-Khwarizmi's 9th-century treatise.

Algebra exists because:
- **Unknown quantities** must be expressed symbolically
- **Relationships** between quantities need compact representation
- **General solutions** eliminate solving the same problem repeatedly
- **Abstraction** enables reasoning about structure rather than numbers

Without algebra there would be no physics equations, no engineering formulas, no computer algorithms — just arithmetic on specific numbers.
