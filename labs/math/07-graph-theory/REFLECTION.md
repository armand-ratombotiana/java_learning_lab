# Reflection on Graph Theory

## Self-Assessment

- Can I implement BFS and DFS from memory?
- Do I understand when to use Dijkstra vs Bellman-Ford vs Floyd-Warshall?
- Can I detect cycles in both directed and undirected graphs?
- Do I know how to find MST, shortest paths, and topological order?
- Can I choose the right graph representation for the problem?

## Growth Areas

- **Network flow**: max flow, min cut, bipartite matching
- **Spectral graph theory**: eigenvalues of adjacency matrix
- **Graph neural networks**: ML on graph-structured data
- **Hypergraphs**: edges connecting more than 2 vertices

## Key Insight

Graphs are everywhere. Every software engineer works with graphs daily — dependency graphs, call graphs, data flow graphs, object graphs. Recognizing the graph structure in a problem is often the key to solving it efficiently.
