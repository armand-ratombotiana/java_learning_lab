# Reflection on Combinatorics

## Self-Assessment

- Can I distinguish permutation vs combination problems?
- Do I understand when to apply inclusion-exclusion?
- Can I write code to generate permutations and combinations?
- Do I recognize when a problem involves Catalan numbers or Stirling numbers?
- Can I compute binomial coefficients without overflow?

## Growth Areas

- **Generating functions**: powerful tool for counting and recurrences
- **Burnside's lemma**: counting under symmetry (Pólya enumeration)
- **Asymptotic combinatorics**: approximating large combinatorial counts
- **Probabilistic method**: existence proofs using probability

## Key Insight

Combinatorics is the art of counting without counting. Instead of enumerating every possibility, we find formulas and algorithms that compute the answer efficiently. This is the essence of algorithmic thinking.
