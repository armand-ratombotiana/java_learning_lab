# Reflection on Trigonometry

## Self-Assessment

- Can I convert between degrees and radians without thinking?
- Do I understand the unit circle and why $\sin^2 + \cos^2 = 1$?
- Can I derive $\sin(A + B)$ and $\cos(A + B)$?
- Do I know when to use `atan2` vs `atan`?
- Can I implement a basic Fourier transform?

## Growth Areas

- **Hyperbolic functions**: $\sinh, \cosh, \tanh$ and their applications
- **Spherical trigonometry**: navigation, astronomy
- **Fourier analysis**: signal processing, convolution theorem
- **Harmonic analysis**: wavelets, filter design

## Key Insight

Trigonometry unifies geometry (triangles), analysis (series), and algebra (complex exponentials). Euler's formula $e^{i\theta} = \cos\theta + i\sin\theta$ is one of the most beautiful connections in all of mathematics.
