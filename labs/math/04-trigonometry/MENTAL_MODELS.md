# Mental Models for Trigonometry

## The Unit Circle

The fundamental model: a circle of radius 1 centered at origin.

$$
x = \cos\theta \quad y = \sin\theta
$$

```
       (0,1)
         │
(-1,0) ──┼── (1,0)
         │
       (0,-1)
```

As $\theta$ goes from $0$ to $2\pi$, the point $( \cos\theta, \sin\theta )$ traces the circle.

## The Wave

Sine and cosine are horizontal/vertical projections of circular motion:

```
sin θ
 1 ─╱╲───╱╲──
 0 ╱  ╲╱  ╲╱ ╲
-1    0  π  2π
```

## The Right Triangle

In a right triangle with angle $\theta$:

- $\sin\theta =$ opposite / hypotenuse
- $\cos\theta =$ adjacent / hypotenuse
- $\tan\theta =$ opposite / adjacent

## Euler's Formula Bridge

$$
e^{i\theta} = \cos\theta + i\sin\theta
$$

This connects trigonometry to complex numbers, exponentials, and differential equations.
