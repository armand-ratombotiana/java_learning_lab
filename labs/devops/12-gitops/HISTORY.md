# History of GitOps

- **2017**: Alexis Richardson (Weaveworks) coins "GitOps" term; Weave Flux released.
- **2018**: ArgoCD released by Intuit as part of the Argo project (Argo Workflows, CD, Rollouts).
- **2019**: Flux v1; ArgoCD graduates from CNCF incubation.
- **2020**: Flux v2 (now Flux) — multi-tenancy, Helm, Kustomize, GitRepository, OCIRepository.
- **2021**: Argo Rollouts (progressive delivery); Flux joins CNCF; GitOps Working Group established.
- **2022**: OpenGitOps standard published (principles, terms, patterns); Flux v0.30+.
- **2023-2024**: ArgoCD v2.8+ — ApplicationSets, multi-cluster, progressive delivery; Flux v2.1+ — OCI support, performance improvements, policy-as-code.

GitOps evolved from a Weaveworks concept to a CNCF-endorsed standard adopted by organizations managing infrastructure at scale.
