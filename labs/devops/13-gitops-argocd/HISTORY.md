# History of GitOps

## Origins (2017)
GitOps was first coined by Alexis Richardson (CEO of Weaveworks) in 2017. The concept emerged from the principles of Infrastructure as Code and the operational patterns developed at companies like Google, Netflix, and Facebook.

## Weave Flux (2017)
The first GitOps operator was Weave Flux, developed by Weaveworks. It implemented the core GitOps pattern: pull-based deployments from Git to Kubernetes. Flux demonstrated the viability of the approach in production environments.

## ArgoCD (2018)
ArgoCD was developed by Applatix (later acquired by Intuit) as an open-source GitOps CD tool for Kubernetes. It was donated to the CNCF and quickly gained adoption due to its rich feature set, web UI, and multi-cluster capabilities.

## CNCF Adoption (2020)
Both ArgoCD and Flux were accepted into the CNCF ecosystem. ArgoCD became a CNCF incubating project, and Flux followed shortly after. This validation led to widespread enterprise adoption.

## Current State (2024+)
GitOps has become the standard deployment methodology for Kubernetes. ArgoCD and Flux are the dominant operators, and the GitOps Working Group maintains the OpenGitOps standard that defines core principles and terminology.
