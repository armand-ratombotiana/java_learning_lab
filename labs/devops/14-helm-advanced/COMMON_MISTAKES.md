# Common Mistakes in Helm Advanced

## Mistake 1: Ignoring Security Best Practices
Many teams skip security hardening during initial setup, leading to vulnerabilities in production.

## Mistake 2: Not Versioning Configurations
Storing configurations outside version control leads to configuration drift and audit challenges.

## Mistake 3: Overlooking Monitoring
Deploying without proper monitoring makes it difficult to detect and diagnose issues.

## Mistake 4: Insufficient Testing
Skipping testing leads to undetected bugs in configuration and deployment logic.

## Mistake 5: Poor Error Handling
Not implementing proper error handling causes cascading failures in production systems.

## Mistake 6: Ignoring Performance
Neglecting performance optimization leads to resource waste and poor user experience.

## Mistake 7: Manual Processes
Relying on manual processes instead of automation increases risk of human error.

## Mistake 8: No Rollback Plan
Deploying without a rollback plan makes it difficult to recover from failed deployments.

## Mistake 9: Insufficient Documentation
Lack of documentation makes it hard for team members to understand and maintain the system.

## Mistake 10: Not Planning for Scale
Designing for current needs without considering future growth leads to costly redesigns.

## Mistake 11: Skipping Disaster Recovery Testing
Having a backup plan without testing it regularly leads to failures during actual disasters.

## Mistake 12: Over-Engineering Solutions
Building overly complex solutions when simpler approaches suffice increases maintenance burden.

## Mistake 13: Ignoring Compliance Requirements
Not considering regulatory and compliance requirements leads to audit failures.

## Mistake 14: Poor Communication
Lack of communication between teams leads to misaligned configurations and operational issues.

## Mistake 15: Not Automating Repetitive Tasks
Manual execution of repetitive tasks increases operational overhead and error rates.
