# History of Data Visualization

## 1700s–1800s: The Founders

- **William Playfair** (1786) invented the line chart, bar chart, and pie chart in *The Commercial and Political Atlas*
- **Florence Nightingale** (1858) used coxcomb charts to show Crimean War mortality — visualization as advocacy
- **Charles Minard** (1869) drew Napoleon's March on Moscow — still the gold standard of multivariate narrative

## 1900s: Statistical Graphics

- John Tukey (1977) introduced the box plot as part of exploratory data analysis
- Jacques Bertin (1967) published *Sémiologie Graphique* — the first systematic theory of visual encoding

## 2000s: Interactive & Programmatic

- Ben Shneiderman (1996): *"Overview first, zoom and filter, then details-on-demand"*
- D3.js (2011) by Mike Bostock made interactive web visualization programmable
- ggplot2 (2007) by Hadley Wickham brought a grammar of graphics to R
- Vega-Lite (2016) brought declarative visualization to JSON
