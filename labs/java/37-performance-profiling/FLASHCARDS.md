# Flashcards

## Card 1
Q: What is the primary goal of good software design?
A: To create maintainable, testable, and well-organized applications.

## Card 2
Q: What does SOLID stand for?
A: Single Responsibility, Open-Closed, Liskov Substitution, Interface Segregation, Dependency Inversion.

## Card 3
Q: What is dependency injection?
A: A pattern where dependencies are provided from outside rather than created internally.

## Card 4
Q: What is the difference between composition and inheritance?
A: Composition has-a relationship; inheritance is-a relationship.

## Card 5
Q: Why use interfaces?
A: To define contracts between components, enabling loose coupling and polymorphism.

## Card 6
Q: What is the single responsibility principle?
A: A class should have exactly one reason to change.

## Card 7
Q: What is the Liskov substitution principle?
A: Subtypes must be substitutable for their base types.

## Card 8
Q: What is the interface segregation principle?
A: Clients should not depend on interfaces they don't use.

## Card 9
Q: What is the dependency inversion principle?
A: Depend on abstractions, not concretions.

## Card 10
Q: What is defensive copying?
A: Creating copies of mutable objects to protect internal state.

## Card 11
Q: What is the difference between checked and unchecked exceptions?
A: Checked exceptions must be declared or caught; unchecked exceptions (RuntimeException) do not.

## Card 12
Q: What is try-with-resources?
A: A Java construct that automatically closes resources implementing AutoCloseable.
