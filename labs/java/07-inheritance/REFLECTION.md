# Inheritance — Reflection

1. How do you decide between inheritance and composition? Do you have a rule of thumb?
2. Have you been burned by a deep inheritance hierarchy? How deep is too deep?
3. How often do you override `equals` and `hashCode`? Do you generate them or write manually?
4. What problems have you encountered with the fragile base class problem?
5. When do you make a class `final`? When do you leave it open for extension?
6. How do constructor chains affect your understanding of object lifecycle?
7. Have you used the Template Method pattern? When is it useful?
8. What's your approach to testing classes that inherit behavior?
9. How do you document inheritance hierarchies for other developers?
10. Do you prefer `@Override` on all overridden methods? Why?
11. Have you used sealed classes (Java 17+)? How do they change hierarchy design?
12. What would you change about Java's inheritance model if you could?
