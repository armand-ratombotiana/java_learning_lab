# Flashcards: Graphs

---

**Q**: What is the difference between BFS and DFS?
**A**: BFS uses a queue (explores level by level); DFS uses a stack (explores depth first).

---

**Q**: What graph representation uses O(V²) memory?
**A**: Adjacency matrix.

---

**Q**: What is Dijkstra's algorithm used for?
**A**: Finding the shortest path from one vertex to all others in a weighted graph.

---

**Q**: What is a DAG?
**A**: Directed Acyclic Graph — a directed graph with no cycles.

---

**Q**: What is topological sort?
**A**: A linear ordering of vertices where every directed edge goes from earlier to later vertex.

---

**Q**: What algorithm finds shortest paths with negative edge weights?
**A**: Bellman-Ford algorithm.

---

**Q**: What is the BFS time complexity?
**A**: O(V + E) with adjacency list.

---

**Q**: What is the property of a bipartite graph?
**A**: Vertices can be partitioned into two sets such that all edges cross between sets.

---

**Q**: Who solved the Seven Bridges of Königsberg problem?
**A**: Leonhard Euler (1736) — considered the birth of graph theory.

---

**Q**: What is an adjacency list?
**A**: An array of lists where adj[i] contains all vertices adjacent to vertex i.
