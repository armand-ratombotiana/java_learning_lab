# History of Skip Lists

## Invention (1989)

William Pugh invented skip lists and published the paper "Skip Lists: A Probabilistic Alternative to Balanced Trees" in Communications of the ACM in 1990 (submitted 1989).

## Adoption

- **1990s**: Widely adopted in academic curricula
- **2000**: Redis added skip lists for sorted sets
- **2000s**: Concurrent skip lists developed
- **2010**: Lock-free skip lists proved correct
- **2020s**: Continued use in modern databases

## Pugh's Motivation

Pugh was frustrated with the complexity of balanced tree implementations. He wanted a data structure that:
1. Could be implemented in a few hours
2. Had the same performance characteristics as balanced trees
3. Could be easily analyzed
4. Supported concurrent access
