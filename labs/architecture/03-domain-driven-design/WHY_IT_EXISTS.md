# Why DDD Exists

## Historical Problems
- **Language gaps** - Business speaks in domain terms, developers in technical terms
- **Complexity management** - Business logic spread across layers
- **Anemic domain model** - Services with procedural code, no domain logic in entities
- **Miscommunication** - Requirements lost in translation between business and tech

## Business Drivers
- Complex domains need deep modeling
- Long-lived systems require maintainable domain logic
- Business rules change frequently and must be explicit
- Competitive advantage through domain expertise

## When DDD Makes Sense
- Complex business domains (finance, healthcare, insurance)
- Systems with evolving requirements
- Long-lived enterprise applications
- Projects requiring close business collaboration
- Microservices with bounded context alignment
